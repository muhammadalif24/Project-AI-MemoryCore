## 1. Customer Segments

- **Pet owners & cat rescuers** (primary): individual cat owners, community feeders, informal rescuers, and small shelters dealing with recurring scabies/mange, ringworm, sporotrichosis, ear mites, and wounds in cats.
- **Human end users**: consumers seeking natural remedies for skin conditions (scabies, ringworm, itching, fungal infections) as an alternative to pharmacy antifungals.
- **Stockists / agents / dropshippers**: a distinct B2B2C segment — regional resellers who buy in bulk (stockists ~RM5,000+ capital) or smaller volume (agents/dropshippers) and resell to end customers in their local area or online.

## 2. Value Propositions

- **100% natural/herbal formulation** — food-grade ingredients (leaves, stems, fruits, roots), positioned as safe and non-toxic vs. synthetic pharma/veterinary treatments.
- **Proven efficacy narrative** — extensive, specific testimonials (named users, named conditions, defined recovery timelines of 1–4 weeks) that directly counter the category's core objection (skepticism about herbal remedy efficacy).
- **Dual-market utility** — one product platform (herbal extracts) serves both animal and human skin/parasitic conditions, extending value from a single R&D investment.
- **Accessibility** — no prescription needed, available through familiar local channels (stockists, Shopee/Lazada) rather than clinics or pharmacies.
- **Founder authenticity** — a genuine, story-driven brand (2 years of dedicated R&D) rather than a generic white-label herbal reseller.

## 3. Channels

- **Owned:** Website (psherbs.com) — Bahasa Melayu, SEO-driven with condition guides and stockist location pages.
- **Social:** Facebook (@psherbs) as primary trust/engagement channel (video, testimonials, direct interaction); Instagram (@psherbs.hq) as secondary/supporting.
- **Marketplace:** Shopee and Lazada — official store plus multiple third-party/stockist listings (currently fragmented).
- **Physical/local:** Distributed network of named stockists and pet shops across Malaysian cities (Kota Bharu, Shah Alam, Changlun, KL, etc.) — local, in-person trust points.

## 4. Customer Relationships

- **Community-driven trust:** built primarily through testimonials, direct Facebook engagement, and word-of-mouth within pet-rescue/feeder communities rather than formal customer service infrastructure.
- **Local personal relationships:** stockists and agents often function as the human "face" of the brand in their region — relationship-based, not self-service.
- **Self-service e-commerce:** Shopee/Lazada listings operate on standard marketplace chat/review mechanics with no visible unified CRM.

## 5. Revenue Streams

- **Direct-to-consumer retail sales** via Shopee, Lazada, and website-referred purchases (per-unit product sales: sprays, creams, powders).
- **Wholesale/bulk sales to stockists** — larger upfront volume purchases at stockist-tier margins.
- **Reseller margin structure** — agents and dropshippers purchase at wholesale/mid-tier pricing and resell at retail, meaning PS Herbs revenue is a mix of direct retail and tiered wholesale pricing depending on the buyer type.
- *(No visible subscription, service, or licensing revenue — this is a pure product-sales model.)*

## 6. Key Resources

- **Proprietary herbal formulations** (Spray F7/F8/F9, Cream S3, Spray H2, Powder FP1) — the core IP, though currently undocumented/unregistered (no visible patent or NPRA registration).
- **Founder R&D knowledge** — tacit, personal expertise built over ~2 years of formulation testing.
- **Testimonial/content library** — an accumulated bank of trust-building customer stories, a real asset for conversion.
- **Stockist/agent network** — a distributed, multi-city human resource network functioning as informal sales force and local credibility anchors.
- **Brand/domain presence** — psherbs.com and associated social accounts with existing SEO equity in Bahasa Melayu search.

## 7. Key Activities

- **Herbal product formulation and production** (small-batch, likely in-house or via a contracted local producer).
- **Content creation** — testimonials, usage guides, condition-specific SEO articles, social media video content.
- **Stockist/agent recruitment and management** — onboarding new regional resellers, maintaining relationships.
- **Marketplace listing management** across Shopee/Lazada (currently fragmented across multiple sellers).
- **Customer education** — teaching correct product usage for specific conditions (a real activity given the recurring "panduan guna" / how-to-use guide content).

## 8. Key Partnerships

- **Regional stockists, agents, and pet shops** — the backbone of physical distribution and local trust.
- **Marketplace platforms** (Shopee, Lazada) — critical sales infrastructure, though currently a dependency risk (see MBA report Section 7).
- *(Not currently visible, but strategically relevant: no apparent partnerships with veterinary clinics, cat rescue organizations/shelters at a formal level, ingredient suppliers, or regulatory bodies — these represent the gaps flagged as opportunities in the MBA report.)*

## 9. Cost Structure

- **Production costs:** raw herbal ingredient sourcing, formulation, small-batch manufacturing, packaging.
- **Distribution/channel costs:** margin given up to stockists/agents/dropshippers (tiered structure, reportedly 30–50%+ depending on tier), marketplace platform fees (Shopee/Lazada commissions).
- **Content & marketing costs:** website/SEO content production, social media management (largely appears organic/low-cost rather than paid media).
- **Logistics:** shipping/fulfillment for direct e-commerce orders and stockist restocking.
- *(Likely a low fixed-cost, variable-cost-dominant structure typical of an SME herbal products business — no visible large capital assets like factories or retail stores.)*

This canvas is a **structural snapshot** — it's meant to be scanned in one sitting to spot where the business model has gaps (e.g., no formal partnerships in Block 8, revenue streams concentrated in Block 5) and to test "what if" scenarios (e.g., what changes in this canvas if PS Herbs adds an English-language channel, or formalizes rescue-group partnerships).
